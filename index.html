About Me

Welcome to my site! I am an engineer focused on delivering technology solutions in the social impact space. Previously, I worked on breakthrough technology development in machine learning, gastroenterology, and genomics fields. I am passionate about improving and prioritizing accessibility and equity of new and existing technology solutions. My educational background in data science also has me thinking about how to best use data and research methodologies to improve understanding of technology design and delivery.

My Journey



A New Chapter: Focused on Civic Technology @ Code for America

After almost 5 years working in Biotech and Healthcare industries developing software and machine learning products, I have recently pivoted into the Civic Technology application space. I am on small a team building and supporting accessible-first platforms that help low-income individuals get access to flexible cash support.

This opportunity has been really exciting to me for a lot of reasons:

  • The work we are doing has measurable impacts on poverty alleviation.
  • Pilots and programs conducted by Code for America have an impressive history of positively influencing policies and programs in government to do a better job reaching vulnerable populations.
Working towards equity through technology feels like the best possible use of my existing skills, and I'm learning more about social context and engineering here everyday.

Full-Stack Software Engineer @ Matterworks

I boldly joined a ~5 person company (now 15) as their first Software Engineer in 2021, inspired by their mission to revolutionize a scientific area called Metabolomics in desparate need of more scalable workflows and data analysis tools. The solutions we were building involved combining innovations from software, machine learning, and chemistry.

I led the effort of designing and implementing multiple software products from the ground-up in a complex scientific discipline for which I had no prior knowledge or understanding. The most notable product I built was an integrated experiment data design and capture software, which was used daily in the lab to enable much more complex experiments than could be done manually, and at scale with traceability built-in. These efforts were critical to enable technology development in R&D at Matterworks, and to inform future software product strategy.

Software Engineer in Computational Biology @ ReadCoor / 10x Genomics

I continued expanding my knowledge of computer vision and software engineering, while exploring a new area of genomics at ReadCoor and 10x genomics.

ReadCoor spun out of the Wyss Institute at Harvard University to commercialize a novel 3D- fluorescence imaging modality called FISSEQ. I helped develop a FISSEQ-specific sequencing instrument with integrated software for analyzing cell and tissue samples at sub-cellular resolution. I worked on imaging registration research, benchmarking, and implementation for stitching together 2D sample images into 3D, the end-result being localized sequencing run information overlaid onto the 3D images. I also helped to build a highly reliable and scalable cloud-based software infrastructure to run the bioinformatics pipelines that the instrument supported. Unfortunately, my employment was cut short due to an acquisition.

Machine Learning Engineer @ Iterative Health

Breaking into the startup world in Boston, and getting to work on a healthcare-oriented product was my dream! I got to learn from and work alongside incredible minds of MIT students and faculty, gastroenterologists, and healthcare practitioners to improve outcomes in colorectal cancer screening and other gastrointestinal diseases through technology.

I was mostly focused on building the core ML engine for the flagship product at Iterative Scopes called Skout: a system which accurately detected and localized colon polyps in real-time. I also became a very proficient Software Engineer by building various internal products to support machine learning at Iterative Scopes.

University of Rochester

September 2013-May 2017

I earned my B.S. in Data Science, Mathematics and Computer Science from the University of Rochester. I pursued multiple research opportunities here as an undergrad, was a TA for applied statistics courses for 3 years, and was a part of the first class to graduate with this degree. This is where I truly became a multi-disciplinary human and researcher. I also played squash on the women's club team for 4 years, a Rochester-area and University of Rochester favorite sport!

If you would like to learn more about my bioinformatics research, please read my App Note that I published with my PI, Dr. Matthew McCall. Or, if you are curious about the android app I developed for asthma management with Dr. Wendi Heinzelman, you can read a little bit about it the ManageMyCondition paper, and a short description in an interview with me for the Rochester magazine.
